The Physical Dimension
Shane Niemeyer, CSCS
PHYSICAL DIMENSION COACH

The human body is the most complex and elegant physical form in the known universe. As human beings, we occupy and inhabit three distinct yet interdependent states. We are feeling, thinking, physical creatures who happen to be embodied in this biological superorganism we call the human body. By design, our physical form is comprised of a series of integrated systems, each contributing its own unique, vital function to the whole.

Intuitively, we know how important it is to care for our physical body. All mental and emotional processes are transmitted through and governed by physical pathways. Therefore, our mental and emotional lives tend to be a reflection of the physical condition of our body. At FIT, we focus on key areas of consensus within the scientific community concerning core issues related to the physical dimension. Many of these issues are found at the intersection of the public health and exercise science fields.

Within the FIT Physical dimension, there are 5 key subcategories: Nutrition, Activity Level, Sleep, Self-Image, and Social components as they relate to the body. These subcategories greatly influence the quality and content of our lives. By strengthening ourselves in these core areas, we enrich and optimize our experience as human beings.

Currently, we are in the midst of a looming healthcare crisis, much of which, is completely preventable or reversible. We are one of the wealthiest nations in the world, yet, simultaneously, one of the most unFIT. There is an ocean of discontent and poor health in this country, and we see this trend continue as other countries advance. It is important to remember that we do not need to remain this way. We can choose different, we can choose better, we can become more FIT. Not just physically, but across all four key life dimensions. Join us on the journey to becoming Physically FIT.
